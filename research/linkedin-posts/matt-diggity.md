## **Post 1 Extract:**
How we outranked the original manufacturer of a product category in one of the most trust-sensitive eCommerce niches online.

By May 2026:
- Organic sessions: +59.6% YoY (27,638 → 44,109)
- Engaged sessions: +42.99% YoY
- AI Overviews visibility: near zero to 634 high-intent keywords

Here's the 5-step playbook 👇

1️⃣ Unify brand positioning across every touchpoint

In trust-sensitive niches, inconsistent messaging costs you as much as weak SEO.

- Make brand messaging consistent across your homepage, product pages, blog, and landing pages
- Strengthen trust signals: FAQs, policies, reassurance copy, customer support links
- Build internal linking around branded keywords to reinforce priority commercial pages
- Align page copy with the questions users ask during their decision-making process

2️⃣ Fix intent alignment before adding new content

Most underperforming pages suffer from intent mismatch, not thin content.

- Refresh existing content to match what users actually need from that query
- Improve structure, readability, and depth across informational pages
- Build contextual internal links from educational content to conversion pages
- Run a full user-journey audit to find friction and weak CTAs

3️⃣ Build community-driven visibility through Reddit

In trust-sensitive niches, buyers check Reddit before they check Google. AI tools pull heavily from those conversations.

- Participate in threads where users compare similar products and weigh options
- Answer questions around brand legitimacy and product effectiveness
- Use Reddit presence to build credibility outside your own domain
- Pair community engagement with your search and AI Overview presence

4️⃣ Fix cannibalization so your best pages can rank

When multiple pages target the same intent, Google splits authority between them and ranks none of them effectively.

- Audit for keyword and topic overlap across commercial and informational pages
- Consolidate or differentiate overlapping pages so each one holds a distinct focus
- Restructure your hierarchy so content groups logically
- Build clear pathways from informational content to commercial pages

5️⃣ Stack E-E-A-T and Digital PR

Authority signals carry more weight in trust-sensitive niches than anywhere else.

- Expand your About page with real credentials and brand background
- Make author information visible on every piece of content with credentials that prove expertise
- Add publication and update dates for freshness signals
- Implement "Why You Can Trust Us" sections explaining your editorial standards
- Secure brand mentions and editorial links from relevant industry publications

## **Post 3 Extract:**
TIPS FROM THE AGENCY (The Search Initiative)

Last year, Google's Spam Update hit one of our clients hard. By March 2026, we'd grown their organic revenue 170.12% year over year and nearly doubled monthly sessions (1,968 to 3,868).

Here's the seasonal recovery playbook we used.

1️⃣ Audit and rebuild after an algorithm hit

Don't just wait it out - identify what caused the drop and fix it.

🔹 Audit affected pages by commercial value, looking for thin content and over-optimized anchor text.
🔹 Rewrite weak copy with experience-led detail.
🔹 Disavow manipulative backlinks and rebuild authority through editorial links from niche-relevant sites.

2️⃣ Hammer your USP into product pages

Generic descriptions lose buyers already searching for what you sell.

Our client's USP was custom-cut marine non-skid - but their pages didn't target "custom" or "made-to-order" intent.

🔹 Rewrite copy to target customization-intent queries.
🔹 Add structured FAQs, schema markup, and benefit-led headings.
🔹 Restructure internal links so authority flows from blogs into commercial pages.

3️⃣ Time everything around your seasonal peak

When you publish determines whether you rank before demand arrives or after it passes.

Their market peaks March-August, so we front-loaded all work in November-February.

🔹 Publish all updates 6-10 weeks before your demand peak.
🔹 Use year-over-year data, not month-over-month.
🔹 Refresh key landing pages before peak, not during it.

4️⃣ Fix your Google Merchant Center feed

Most Shopping issues come from feed errors, not bid strategy.

🔹 Audit for missing attributes, incorrect product types, and unoptimized titles.
🔹 Rewrite product titles with primary keywords and key specifications.
🔹 Populate every required attribute and add custom labels for future segmentation.

5️⃣ Replace ad-hoc blogging with topical clusters

Random blog posts don't build authority - structured clusters do.

🔹 Research long-tail queries and build a content calendar around topic clusters.
🔹 Link every article to relevant product and category pages.
🔹 Use clear headings and FAQ schema for AI search visibility.

The results after 5 months:

📈 Organic sessions: 1,968 → 3,868 (+96.54% YoY)
📈 Organic revenue: +170.12% YoY
