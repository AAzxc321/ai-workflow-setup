# Kevin Indig

## Why this expert was selected

Kevin Indig was selected because his work connects AI search, SEO strategy, organic growth, user behavior, measurement, and business impact. For this research, the useful angle is how AI search changes content visibility, citations, authority signals, search behavior, and the way SEO teams measure results.

## Research angle

Use Kevin Indig as a source for:

- AI search visibility and citation behavior.
- SEO measurement when traffic and clicks become less reliable.
- How AI Overviews and AI Mode change user behavior.
- Content structure and citation likelihood in AI-generated answers.
- Brand and third-party authority signals in AI search.
- Durable AI SEO strategy beyond short-term tactics.
- Organic growth planning in an AI-first search environment.

## How these sources support the research

These sources show Kevin Indig’s approach to AI search as a measurement, behavior, and strategy problem. The materials are useful for understanding how AI systems change what content gets cited, how users behave differently in AI-assisted search experiences, and how SEO teams can adapt content planning around prompts, citations, authority, and business impact.

The strongest pattern across these sources is that AI SEO cannot be reduced to publishing more content. Content needs to be specific, focused, evidence-backed, citation-worthy, and connected to how users actually search and make decisions inside AI-driven search environments.
