# Lily Ray

## Why this expert was selected

Lily Ray was selected because her work focuses on AI search, Google algorithm updates, E-E-A-T, content quality, and search visibility risk. For this research, the useful angle is how AI-assisted content and GEO/AEO tactics interact with Google’s quality systems, brand authority, and long-term organic visibility.

## Research angle

Use Lily Ray as a source for:

- AI search and SEO overlap.
- GEO/AEO hype vs. practical SEO fundamentals.
- Risks of scaled AI content.
- Google algorithm updates and content quality.
- E-E-A-T and source trust in AI search.
- The relationship between organic rankings and AI search visibility.
- Long-term risk in shortcut-based AI SEO strategies.

## Additional materials

### 1. GEO, AEO, LLMO: Separating Fact from Fiction & How to Win in AI Search

- **URL:** https://www.amsive.com/insights/seo/geo-aeo-llmo-separating-fact-from-fiction-how-to-win-in-ai-search/
- **Date:** 2025-11-12
- **Source type:** Presentation / transcript / AI search framework
- **Annotation:** Lily Ray’s MozCon 2025 presentation adapted into an article and transcript. It separates AI search hype from practical reality and argues that many AI search tactics are still rooted in existing SEO fundamentals: useful content, technical precision, brand authority, and trustworthy information.
- **Relevance:** Strongest source for Lily’s overall position on AI search. It is useful for framing GEO, AEO, and LLMO as extensions of SEO rather than replacements for it.

### 2. Your GEO Strategy Might Be Destroying Your SEO

- **URL:** https://lilyraynyc.substack.com/p/your-geo-strategy-might-be-destroying
- **Date:** 2026-03-18
- **Source type:** Article / AI search risk analysis
- **Annotation:** Lily Ray argues that some tactics designed to win in AI search can damage the SEO foundation that AI systems often rely on for retrieval. The article discusses risky GEO tactics such as rapid AI content scaling, artificial refreshing, self-promotional listicles, prompt-injection-style “summarize with AI” buttons, and excessive comparison pages.
- **Relevance:** Highly relevant because it connects AI search visibility to long-term SEO health. It is useful for evaluating whether AI-powered content workflows create durable value or short-term visibility that later collapses.

### 3. It Works Until It Doesn’t: AI Content Strategies That Backfire

- **URL:** https://lilyraynyc.substack.com/p/it-works-until-it-doesnt-ai-content-risks
- **Date:** 2026-05-13
- **Source type:** Article / AI content risk analysis
- **Annotation:** Lily Ray analyzes more than 220 websites publicly associated with AI content creation, automation, or scaling platforms and identifies a pattern where initial SEO or AI-search gains can later reverse.
- **Relevance:** Most useful source for the “do not rely on low-quality automated content” part of the research objective. It gives a practical warning against treating AI content scale as a sustainable SEO strategy without originality, review, quality control, and long-term visibility checks.

## How these sources support the research

These sources show Lily Ray’s approach to AI search and AI content through the lens of risk, quality, and long-term visibility. The materials are useful for understanding why AI-powered SEO content production should not be reduced to scale, automation, or GEO shortcuts.

The strongest pattern across these sources is that AI search visibility still depends on the same foundations that make content trustworthy and retrievable in traditional search: strong SEO performance, technical accessibility, useful content, credible sources, and real authority. Lily’s work is especially useful as a counterweight to overly aggressive AI-content and AI-search tactics.
