# AI Workflow Setup

## Objective

This repository documents the setup process completed as part of an assessment. The objective was to install the required AI tools, configure the development environment, collect research on a selected topic, and organize the material in a GitHub repository.

The research topic selected for this assessment was **AI-powered SEO content production**.

---

## Research Collection

The research goal was to understand how experienced SEO and content practitioners use AI to support research, content planning, optimization, production, distribution, and AI search visibility without relying on low-quality automated content.

I selected 10 experts because they actively practice, publish, test, teach, or operate in areas related to SEO, AI search, content operations, technical SEO, content automation, editorial quality, and distribution.

### What Was Collected

The research materials are organized as follows:

- `research/sources.md` — master source list with all 10 experts, links, dates, source types, and brief annotations.
- `research/linkedin-posts/` — manually collected LinkedIn posts organized by expert.
- `research/youtube-transcripts/` — YouTube transcripts collected with a Python transcript workflow and organized by expert/video.
- Source list: `videos.csv`
- Fetch script: `fetch_youtube_transcripts.py`
- Transcript structure: one expert folder per source/person, with Markdown transcript files inside.
- `research/other/` — additional articles, case studies, podcast pages, frameworks, guides, and supporting materials organized by expert.

### Why These Experts Were Chosen

The selected experts cover different parts of AI-powered SEO content production:

- Aleyda Solís, Bernard Huang, Kevin Indig, Lily Ray, Michael King, and Matt Diggity were included for AI search optimization, AEO/GEO, technical SEO, content quality, AI visibility, citations, and measurement.
- Gael Breton and Julian Goldie were included for practical AI SEO automation, content workflows, AI agents, and execution-heavy SEO systems.
- Ross Simmonds was included for content distribution, repurposing, GEO, and AI-assisted distribution systems.
- Ryan Law was included for AI-assisted editorial workflows, content engineering, content quality, and practical content production systems.

Together, the collected sources provide a research base for understanding how AI can support SEO content production while still requiring human strategy, editorial judgment, source verification, and quality control.

## Research Process

Before installing any software, I verified that the request was legitimate by checking the company, reviewing the official websites, and comparing the hiring process with publicly available information.

During the setup and research process, I used ChatGPT to help organize the workflow and explain unfamiliar steps. I also used Google Search, Google's AI Overview, YouTube, official documentation, expert websites, podcasts, articles, and public social content to verify information and collect research materials.

Before moving on to each step, I manually checked the information to ensure everything was accurate and that no mistakes had been made.

For YouTube transcripts, I used a Python-based transcript workflow to collect captions/transcripts without downloading video files. For LinkedIn posts, I manually collected relevant posts and organized them by expert. For additional materials, I collected articles, case studies, podcast pages, frameworks, guides, and other supporting sources.

## Issues Encountered

Cursor initially opened in the **Cursor Agents** view instead of the standard editor. Because of this, the **Extensions** menu mentioned in the instructions was not visible.

I searched for the issue using Google Search, Google's AI Overview, and YouTube. Most of the available information referred to the updated interface, which initially made it unclear where extensions could be managed.

After approximately ten minutes of troubleshooting, I restarted Cursor and opened a new workspace. This time the standard editor loaded correctly, displaying the full sidebar and the Extensions menu. From there, I installed Codex without any further issues.

Another issue came up when collecting YouTube transcripts. The transcript workflow initially could not run because Python was not installed locally. After installing Python, the transcript script ran successfully and collected the YouTube transcripts without downloading video files.

## Takeaways

This exercise reinforced the importance of verifying information before taking action.

Rather than relying on a single source, I compared information across multiple sources, confirmed that the request was legitimate, and validated each step before proceeding. This is the same approach I generally apply when researching information or making marketing decisions.

The research process also showed that AI can be useful for organizing workflows, troubleshooting technical steps, and structuring research, but the final quality still depends on manual review, source selection, and judgment.
